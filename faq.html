Who is Candy Loving?

Candy Loving is an American model and actress who gained fame as Playboy's Playmate of the Month in January 1979, which coincided with the magazine's 25th anniversary celebration. Born Candis Loving on September 4, 1956, in Oswego, Kansas, she became one of the most recognizable Playmates of her era. At 18 years old when she appeared in Playboy, she represented a particular aesthetic that resonated with millions of readers. Her pictorial was shot by renowned Playboy photographer Richard Fegley and became one of the best-selling issues in the magazine's history. Beyond her centerfold appearance, Candy made various television appearances and continued modeling work throughout the early 1980s before eventually stepping away from public life to pursue privacy.

What is Candy Loving known for?

Candy Loving is best known for her appearance in Playboy magazine and her work as a glamour model in the late 1970s and early 1980s. Her January 1979 Playmate of the Month appearance was particularly significant because it marked Playboy's 25th anniversary, making her the anniversary Playmate for this milestone celebration. This special designation meant her pictorial received extra promotion and attention, contributing to exceptional newsstand sales. Beyond Playboy, she became known for television appearances on variety shows and talk shows of the era, where her natural charisma and girl-next-door appeal made her a popular guest. She also participated in Playboy promotional events and made personal appearances throughout the United States, building a substantial fan base that extended beyond magazine readers.

When did Candy Loving appear in Playboy?

Candy Loving appeared as Playboy's Playmate of the Month in January 1979 when she was 18 years old. This timing was strategically significant as January 1979 marked exactly 25 years since Playboy's first issue in December 1953, making her the official 25th Anniversary Playmate. The issue hit newsstands in late December 1978, becoming one of the magazine's best-selling editions with circulation exceeding the monthly average of 5.6 million copies. Her pictorial was shot in 1978 when she was still attending the University of Oklahoma, and the magazine featured extensive interview content alongside the photographs. The January 1979 issue has since become a highly collectible edition, with pristine copies commanding premium prices among Playboy memorabilia collectors.

By the mid-1980s, she began withdrawing from public life, choosing privacy over continued celebrity. Reports indicate she relocated to Florida where she has lived quietly for several decades, successfully transitioning away from her former fame and maintaining a private existence that she has protected from media intrusion.
